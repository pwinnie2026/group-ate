Background

Commuter students are denied the option to purchase a meal plan at UCLA, thus causing them to face the struggle of arranging their own meals while attending classes on campus. Observing UCLA’s limited number of communal refrigeration (storage) and microwave (reheating) appliances, many commuter students are left to navigate and prepare their meals independently.


With 43% of UCLA’s off-campus population commuting more than an hour each way, commuter students may choose to stay on-campus late to beat evening traffic (Lopez, LA Times) . UCLA’s BruinHub, opened in 2021, provides only one fridge and microwave, and while it serves as a great start to providing resources for commuters, it is difficult to accommodate for almost 30,000 undergraduate and graduate commuters (Hallisey, UCLA transportation).


Housed in the Wooden Center, students’ meal prep resources are “out of the way of classes” (Rajha, BruinLife) . Through our mapplication, we hope to provide a platform for UCLA commuter students to have their voices heard, amplifying their experiences with meal prepping.




Who is Being Empowered and How?

We aim to empower UCLA commuter students living off-campus by providing a space to voice their comments and concerns regarding available meal prep resources on-campus and the surrounding region. Our mapplication’s survey and map will be piloted through our AAS 191A class population to assess usability. After piloting, we intend to extend our platform to the wider UCLA commuter population through UCLA’s Commuter Support and Programs office and BruinHub. Specifically, the Bruin Commuter Ambassadors, dedicated towards the development and advocacy of resources to meet community needs, plans large events including New Commuter Student Welcome, Commuter Awareness Day, and quarterly Commuter Breakfast, Lunches, and Dinners.


Naturally due to varying commutes, our respondents’ time and schedules should be respected and prioritized in our survey design. Therefore, the survey should take no more than 10 minutes depending on the thoroughness of the respondent’s answers. The Commuter Support and Programs office’s newsletter, Instagram, and Facebook Group provide additional outreach methods that allow for consistent, periodic reminders to fill out the survey should students forget or need additional time. However, we do acknowledge the potential bias a strictly online survey could pose since there are students that are not as technologically literate, not native English speakers, or have trouble accessing online resources.




Long Term Impact

We hope the data from our mapplication will be used by community organizations like cityLAB, the organization that initiated the creation of BruinHub, and other commuter support groups to identify and address the struggles commuters who meal prep face due to the lack of convenient and accessible locations for food storage and reheating, but it can highlight the systemic gaps and inequalities in resource allocation. CityLAB and other organizations can use the information to advocate for structural changes such as pressuring university administration to prioritize funding for more accessible meal-prep resources and equitable food storage solutions.


The information on the mapplication would also be accessible to current and future students, empowering them to navigate and demand better food storage spaces on campus. Overall, we hope the project can provide an outlet for the community to share on-campus issues related to meal-prepping and food accessibility faced by students who must plan and bring their meals from home.
